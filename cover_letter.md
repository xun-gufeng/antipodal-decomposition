# Cover Letter — PNAS Direct Submission

---

Dear Editor,

I am pleased to submit the manuscript "Antipodal Complementarity in a 3×3 Topological Partition of China's Climate: A Three-Contribution Decomposition" for consideration as a Research Report in PNAS.

This manuscript addresses a fundamental question in spatial statistics: when a continuous geographic field is partitioned into discrete regions, can a specific partitioning rule carry detectable statistical structure beyond what arbitrary partitions would produce? We answer this question affirmatively using a striking test case.

The 3×3 azimuthal partition of China centered on Luoyang, when paired at 180° separation, yields seasonal temperature amplitude complementarity that ranks 1st among all 105 possible pairing schemes. We decompose this optimality into three nested, non-separable contributions: a dominant latitude gradient (91.7% variance), the antipodal pairing principle itself (the only 4/4 antipodal scheme among 105), and an east–west asymmetry from monsoon–elevation contrast (2.0×, p < 0.001). A three-tiered null model hierarchy confirms specificity at p ≤ 0.010 across all tiers. Results are robust across 18 sliding 30-year windows, all 12 calendar months (ranks ≤6/105), and five NCEP climate variables (ranks 1–74), with complementarity tracking latitude dependence—confirming that the latitude gradient is the primary physical driver.

Two features distinguish this work from conventional climate regionalization. First, the partition is fully prespecified (only 2 free parameters: center and radius), yet achieves optimality in complete enumeration—unlike data-driven methods that optimize fit within flexible classes. Second, the decomposition and null model frameworks are general-purpose tools applicable to any discrete spatial partition, from epidemiological surveillance to ecological zoning.

The partitioning rule coincides with the Luoshu (洛书) configuration of Chinese mathematical tradition—the unique 3×3 normal magic square. This historical connection is discussed in the manuscript but is not the basis of the scientific claims; the statistical results stand independently of cultural context.
